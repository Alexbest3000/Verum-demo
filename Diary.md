Founder’s Log: 24-Hour Rapid Iteration & Pivot Report

Period: January 26–27, 2026
I. Initial Deployment & Data Collection (Jan 26, 18:00 – Jan 27, 04:45)

    The Launch: On January 26 at 18:00, I deployed our first targeted ad campaign for the US and Australian markets.

    The Problem: By the following morning, analytics showed over 60 profile visits and significant site traffic, but zero analysis executions.

    Critical Insight: We identified a massive friction point in the mobile user journey. Users coming from Instagram Reels are in a "passive browsing" state; they do not have suspicious files or deepfake-suspect media ready in their phone galleries to upload. The requirement to "Drag & Drop" or provide personal media was an immediate bounce trigger.

II. The 3-Hour Product Pivot (Jan 27, 04:45 – 07:40)

Between 04:45 and 07:40 I re-engineered the core user experience to eliminate the "no-file" barrier:

    UX Overhaul: We removed the complex "Drag & Drop" and "Upload" instructions which were ill-suited for mobile-first traffic.

    "Instant Gratification" Feature: I implemented a "TRY DEMO SAMPLE" button. This allows users to test the engine's capabilities with a pre-loaded "Image for Free" sample.

    Functional Validation: I verified this new flow by analyzing a strategic quote from NVIDIA’s Jensen Huang. The system successfully moved beyond simple detection, uncovering "Negotiation Vulnerabilities" and "OPSEC failures," proving the tool's value as a high-level strategic asset.

    Funnel Correction: I identified that my initial campaign was optimized for "Profile Visits," forcing users through a redundant step (Ad → IG Profile → Link-in-bio → Site).

    New Deployment: I terminated the inefficient campaign and launched a Direct-to-Web campaign. 100% of our ad spend is now directed straight to the functional interface at verum.live, drastically reducing CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost).

Executive Summary

In less than 24 hours, we executed a full-stack pivot: we corrected a flawed marketing funnel, solved a major UX friction point by adding the Demo mode, and re-branded the value proposition from a generic "security" promise to a high-utility "Bullshit Detector". This level of velocity demonstrates our ability to interpret market data and adapt the product in real-time
